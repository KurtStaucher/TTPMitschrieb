\chapter{Interacting fields}

In quantum electro dynamics the full Lagrangian is given as
\[ \LL =-\frac{1}{4} F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu} - \frac{\lambda}{2} ( \p A)^2 + \bar\psi ( i\slashed \p - m ) \psi - q \bar \psi \gamma^\mu \psi A_\mu\]
For simplicity we look at only one fermion with arbitrary charge $q$. The first two terms are being described by coupled harmonic oscillators. We then can parametrizise the other terms as an interacting term $\LL_I$ and is it as a perubation to the rest, $\LL _0$.\\
We then have a time dependant pertubation theory for $H = H_0 + H_I$ with $H_I = - \int \dd ^3 x \LL_I(x)$ .\\
We can use the same splitting for other models, for example can we use $\LL_0$ for terms up to quadratic int he fields and $\LL_I$ for any terms with three or more firelds. We are sometimes foing to use the $\phi^4$ theory as an example where the Lagrangian is
\[ \LL = \frac{1}{2} ( \p_\mu \phi) (\p^\mu \phi) - \frac{m^2}{2} \phi^2 - \frac{\lambda}{4} \phi^4\]
